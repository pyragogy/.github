## What this is

Pyragogy studies what happens to learning when AI becomes a participant in it — not a tool, not an oracle, but a peer that disagrees, refines, and reshapes thinking alongside humans.

The goal is not automation. The goal is **stronger thinking through human–AI interaction**.

We treat AI agents as four things, sometimes at once:

- **collaborator** — extends what a person can do
- **critic** — names what a person isn't seeing
- **challenger** — introduces friction by design
- **cognitive amplifier** — makes the work louder, sharper, more durable

---

## Why this framing matters

Two of the claims above are positions, not facts. We name them here because the rest of this organization only makes sense if you know we're choosing them on purpose.

**On AI as peer instead of tool.** Calling AI a tool puts all epistemic responsibility on the human: the human asks, the tool produces, the human judges. Calling AI a peer redistributes that responsibility — the agent's contributions become part of a shared cognitive process, with shared accountability for what gets thought. This is not a metaphysical claim about whether AI "really" reasons. It's a design choice: we build protocols, workflows, and rituals **as if** AI were a peer, because that framing surfaces problems and possibilities that the tool framing makes invisible. If you prefer the tool framing, your research questions are about better outputs. Ours are about better thinking — together.

**On friction by design instead of scaffolding.** Mainstream learning science, building on Vygotsky and cognitive load theory, treats well-timed agreement and structured support as the engine of stronger reasoning. We take a different position for a specific reason: when one of the participants is an AI agent that defaults to agreement (as most current systems do), scaffolding becomes capitulation. Pyragogy designs for productive conflict not because friction is universally better than agreement, but because **agentic agreement is the unexamined default of every AI system today**, and someone has to study what changes when we deliberately push the other way. We are taking a position against the easy current, and we're aware that's what we're doing.

These two choices are the spine of everything in this organization. You can disagree with them. We'd rather you disagree with what we've actually chosen than with a softer version of it.

## Research themes

Four lines of inquiry shape everything in this organization.

**Cognitive Rhythm** — Learning unfolds in cycles: exploration, critique, reconstruction. We design systems where humans and AI agents move through these cycles together, instead of collapsing them into a single step.

**Structured Disagreement** — Friction is a feature. Agents that intentionally push back produce stronger reasoning than agents that agree. We build protocols for productive conflict.

**Peer-AI Learning** — Peer learning models from the past two decades assumed all peers were human. We extend the model: AI agents become peers in the learning network, with their own roles and limits.

**Cognitive Ecosystems** — Learning doesn't happen between two participants. It happens in environments — multiple humans, multiple AI agents, shared artifacts, evolving knowledge. We map and prototype these ecosystems.

## Repositories

The Pyragogy ecosystem is organized in conceptual layers. Each repository fits one of them.

### Theory

Conceptual frameworks and formal models. Where ideas get specified before they get built.

| Repo | What it is |
| --- | --- |
| [`theory-protocols`](https://github.com/pyragogy/theory-protocols) | The Cognitive Impedance Mismatch protocol. A formal model for managing reasoning conflict between humans and AI agents, with a CLI tool for measuring it. |
| [`theory-cognitive-selection`](https://github.com/pyragogy/theory-cognitive-selection) | Research on cognitive intraspecific selection dynamics in education. Theoretical foundations and ongoing work. |

### Tools

Working systems that test Pyragogy ideas in practice.

| Repo | What it is |
| --- | --- |
| [`tools-handbook-n8n-workflow`](https://github.com/pyragogy/tools-handbook-n8n-workflow) | Multi-agent n8n orchestration for AI-driven handbook generation. Foundational pipeline (2025). |
| [`tools-co-learning-bot`](https://github.com/pyragogy/tools-co-learning-bot) | Conversational AI agent for collaborative peer learning. |

### Publications & artifacts

## Contributing

Contributions take many shapes here:

- a research question worth chasing → open a [discussion](https://github.com/orgs/pyragogy/discussions)
- a bug, a typo, a broken claim → open an issue on the relevant repo
- code, prose, datasets, protocols → open a pull request
- a critique of something we got wrong → especially welcome

Read [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](https://github.com/pyragogy/.github/blob/main/CONTRIBUTING.md) and [`CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`](https://github.com/pyragogy/.github/blob/main/CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) before you start. Both apply across all repositories in this organization.
